There's a temptation when building a personal site to make it impressive. Animations, gradients, parallax scrolling, 3D elements. But impressive to whom?

> The ability to simplify means to eliminate the unnecessary so that the necessary may speak.

The best personal websites I've encountered share a common trait: they get out of the way of the content. Paul Graham's site is pure text. Dan Abramov's blog is a white page with words. Lee Robinson's site is centered text with generous whitespace.

## Typography Does the Work

When you strip away visual complexity, typography becomes your entire design system. The right serif font at the right size with the right line height creates a reading experience that feels deliberate and considered.

Choose one serif for body text, one sans-serif for headings, and one monospace for code. That's your entire type system.

## Whitespace as a Feature

Empty space isn't wasted space. It's breathing room. It guides the eye, separates ideas, and creates a rhythm to the page. A cramped page with great content still feels exhausting to read.

## The Speed Advantage

Minimal design has a practical benefit too. Fewer assets means faster loads. No JavaScript animations means no layout shifts. A 50KB page loads instantly on any connection.

For a personal site, speed is respect for your reader's time.
